Chris Kissel

I am passionate about improving the American healthcare system by promoting transparency and improving the emotional relationships people have with their healthcare.

Nothing fulfills me more than transforming complex, complicated ideas into clear, actionable strategies that help people live longer, healthier lives. Collaboration is key, and I strive to connect manufactures, payers, providers and consumers through solving multifaceted problems with technology.

Key areas of interest: mobile, micro-location context, wearables, big data, social, cloud computing, ecommerce & 3-D printing.

Based in NYC, I've spoken on healthcare topics on SiriusXM's The Focus Group, am the owner of a YouTube health channel, was the winner of Merck’s Social Health Hack, and am a former speaker at NY’s Social Media Week and the HiMSS and Biotech conferences. I'm also a community producer at BRIC Media Arts Center.

I have led the strategic and tactical development of web, mobile and ecommerce properties a for a variety of businesses including: UnitedHealthcare, Merck, J&J, and others.

My formal education is focused in research, systems thinking and business. I hold a BA in Sociology and Anthropology from the University of Wisconsin-Stevens Point, an MA in Sociology from Minnesota State University; I am PhD/ABD in Sociology from the London School of Economics, and have an MBA from Villanova University.
